The social layer of regeneration

We dream of regenerative production as a real, lasting change. To get there, we build the social dimension that sustains it: education, outreach and traceability.

Explore our programs Get involved
What we do

Regeneration takes more than good practices: it takes a culture to sustain it

We work on three fronts so that change doesn't depend on technique alone, but on the people and communities who make it possible.

01

Education

We train farms and civil society in the regenerative paradigm and its practices.

02

Outreach

We create connections between countryside and city, regenerative and conventional producers, the private sector and institutions.

03

Social traceability

We measure the social impact of farms, inside and beyond the gates.

How we do it

We create fertile social ground for regeneration to thrive

Inside the gates

On the farm

  • Training and support with regenerative organization models.
  • Job opportunities: a job board and internships for young people on regenerative farms.
  • Audit and evaluation of social impact on the farm.
Beyond the gates

In society

  • Local Regeneras: gatherings that give regeneration visibility in every territory.
  • Semilla: kids learning regeneration at school.
  • Communication: putting regeneration on the public agenda.
  • Partnerships with municipalities, universities and institutions.

Where we come from

The foundation was born from the ecosystem that has been regenerating grasslands in South America for over two decades: Ovis 21, Ruuts, Escuela de Regeneración and the Savory network. That journey taught us something simple: technique alone is not enough. Without trained people, connected communities and trust you can measure, regeneration doesn't thrive. Fundación Regenera exists to build that layer.
